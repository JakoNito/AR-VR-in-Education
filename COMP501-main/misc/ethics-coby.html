Ethical Issues:

Virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) simulations in flight simulation and education is a growing field. With the quick growth of this field there have been questions behind the ethics. Firstly, simulations can be a cause of trainers to lose their jobs. With the ever-growing technology behind these simulations the need for people to help with these will be reduced as the software will know what to do. Also, if future / trainee pilots have only ever practiced and trained with these simulations, it could create a false sense of security in their actual ability. Flight simulations can also have adverse health effects. There have been studies where people can get damage such as eye strain from virtual reality due to extended periods of us. On the other hand, these simulations have helped decrease human error during flight. Simulations are also more sustainable with updates, then needing to purchase the latest plane for practice. Environmentally wise, the aviation industry is the leading transportation field for producing emissions. Using simulations to teach flying is a great sustainable and environmentally friendly way to help decrease these emissions. Simulations to help create new aircraft to prevent safety concerns when testing new planes, as these new aircraft can be simulated flying before ever being built (Allerton, 2009, p. 13) thus also helping with the environmental issues of running and building aircraft. To continue using VR and AR simulations in education it is important to understand that practice in these simulations does not always convert to real life skills and that there will always need to be an aspect of real-life training.
